Ralph Waldo Emerson

(1803 - 1882)

"I have seen Emerson, the first man I have ever seen," wrote George Eliot in her diary many years ago. Carlyle uses similar expressions in his letters at least a score of times. Sentences like the following appear very often:— "It remains true and will remain, what I have often told you, that properly there is no voice in this world which is completely human to me but your voice only." Again:— "In the whole world I hardly get to my spoken human word any other word of response that is authentically human. God help us, this is growing a very lonely place, this distracted dog-kennel of a world."
